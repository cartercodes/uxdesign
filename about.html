Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific Ocean. With its pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and rich cultural heritage, Taniti offers visitors a unique blend of relaxation, adventure, and authentic island experiences. Our community welcomes tourists while maintaining our traditional way of life and natural environment.

Our Culture & Traditions

The people of Taniti have maintained their rich cultural heritage for generations. Our traditional dances, music, and crafts continue to be an integral part of daily life, while our festivals celebrate the island's connection to the ocean and land.

Visitors are invited to experience our traditions through cultural performances, workshops, and community events that showcase the authentic spirit of Taniti.

Essential Travel Information

Airport Access

Taniti is served by a small airport that connects to major Pacific rim cities. Direct flights are available from Los Angeles and Sydney.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors can stay up to 30 days without a visa. Check our detailed visa guide for specific country requirements.

Currency

The local currency is the Taniti Dollar (TD). Major credit cards are widely accepted, and currency exchange is available at the airport and hotels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time to visit Taniti?
The best time to visit Taniti is during the dry season (April to October) when the weather is most pleasant. However, our tropical climate means you can enjoy the island year-round.
How do I get around the island?
Taniti offers various transportation options including rental cars, bikes, and local buses. Many visitors also enjoy our guided tours which provide transportation to major attractions.
Is English widely spoken?
While Tanitian is our official language, English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Many locals are bilingual or multilingual.
